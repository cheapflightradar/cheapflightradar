## Design Context

### Users
Anyone curious about travel — getting inspired, planning a trip, or looking for tips on destinations, tools, and costs. Not Texas-specific; a global audience of casual and enthusiast travelers who want actionable ideas and honest guidance. They arrive in discovery mode: "where should I go next?" or "how do I make this work on my budget?"

### Brand Personality
Friendly, easy-going, genuinely helpful. Not a deal-hunting tool — a travel blog with a human voice. Think of a well-traveled friend who gives you real advice, not a corporate travel portal.

Three words: **Warm. Clear. Wanderlusty.**

Emotional goal: readers should feel excited and capable — "I can actually do this trip."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Supports both light and dark mode. Light is the default; dark should feel calm and atmospheric, not gloomy.
- **Visual tone**: Minimal with intentional accents. Clean whitespace is the base; color and texture appear where they earn it — a standout card, a highlighted quote, a featured destination. Not sterile minimal, not loud.
- **Typography**: Distinctive display font for headlines (something with personality — editorial, slightly literary). Clean readable body font. Avoid system defaults, avoid Inter/Roboto.
- **Color**: One warm accent (not generic emerald-green) used sparingly and purposefully. Neutral palette that leans slightly warm, not cold gray. Avoid the AI palette: no cyan-on-dark, no purple gradients, no neon glows.
- **Layout**: Varied card sizes to signal editorial curation — a featured post, secondary posts, smaller tiles. Not identical card grids. Left-aligned, asymmetric where it feels natural.
- **Anti-references**: Corporate travel portals (Expedia, Google Flights UI), generic AI blog templates, anything that looks like it was generated in 10 minutes.
- **References**: Going.com (clean, confident), Monocle Travel (editorial, tasteful), a well-designed travel magazine layout.

### Design Principles
1. **Earn the accent** — color and decoration appear only where they add meaning or guide the eye; silence is the default.
2. **Editorial over algorithmic** — vary card sizes, feature posts, create rhythm; resist identical repeating grids.
3. **Globally welcoming** — zero Texas-first framing; the blog is for anyone, anywhere, dreaming of anywhere.
4. **Typography carries personality** — the font choice IS the brand voice; generic fonts = generic blog.
5. **Calm confidence** — the interface doesn't try hard. It trusts its content. No sticky banners fighting for attention, no aggressive CTAs.
